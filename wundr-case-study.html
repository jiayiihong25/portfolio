Wundr: Turning Passive Screen Time into Active Co-Creation

An AI-powered design suite that empowers parents and children to co-create personalized, educational 2D RPGs from study materials.
Competition: UoftHacks13
Role: Design Engineer

Why Wundr?

The theme of UoftHacks13 was identity. While brainstorming, I ran into a shocking statistic: the average middle school child spends 5+ hours online every day. I realized the problem isn’t the time spent, but the passive nature of that time. Most children’s apps are traps to capture attention, not tools to inspire identity creation.

Wundr is the solution. It’s an AI-powered design team that gives children autonomy, allowing them to turn schoolwork alongside their parents into a world they create themselves.

The "IKEA" Effect

The "IKEA effect" is the idea that people value products more if they helped create them. My hypothesis was that if a child were to design their own game rather than play a pre-existing one, their emotional investment in the educational components would skyrocket.

To make this work for a 7-year-old, I had to solve two main pain points:

  • Complexity: Most game engines are far too complex for children.
  • The Parent Gap: Parents struggle to bridge the gap between homework and play time.

To remove "blank page syndrome," I structured the journey into three distinct "Booths" to guide the child through the creative process. I also integrated a microphone feature into every booth to lower the friction of typing for young users.

The Design Booths

  • 1. The Character Designer: Bringing the Hero to Life
    I didn't want children to just pick a skin. Using a Dual-Input Canvas, children can sketch directly on a digital pad. Our system captures their unique handwriting—transforming a blue scribble into a fully animated walking sprite.
  • 2. The World Designer: Engineering an Environment
    The child acts as the stage-setter. By describing an atmosphere (e.g., "I love beaches"), our generative pipeline creates a custom 2D map with specific assets like palm trees and crashing waves. This ensures every study session feels like a new authority over their digital space.
  • 3. The Sound Designer: Crafting the Vibe
    Audio is vital for immersion. The system translates abstract feelings (e.g., "cool, excited tone") into multiple .mp3 options. By choosing the music, the child completes the sensory identity of their game.

The Portal: The Parental Bridge

For parents, the process is simple: they paste study notes (math, history, etc.) into the Portal. I designed a Difficulty Level selector (ages 0 to 11+) to ensures the AI parses the notes into developmentally appropriate "Battle Challenges" within the child's created world.

Conclusion

Wundr achieved exactly what we set out to do: it turned a child from a consumer into a creator. By leveraging the IKEA effect through AI, we transformed "5 hours of screen time" from a statistic of concern into a window of active, identity-building play!
